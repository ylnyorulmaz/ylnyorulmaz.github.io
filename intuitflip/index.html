🪙 IntuitFlip

A subconscious decision-making ritual.

IntuitFlip is a minimalist coin flip experience where the result is random, but your emotional reaction reveals what you already wanted.

Live: intuitflip.com

Type: Progressive Web App (PWA) · Stack: React · Tailwind · Framer Motion · Local-first

Overview

At first glance, IntuitFlip looks simple: enter two options, flip a coin, and receive a result.

But the result is not the point. The signal appears in the split second before the coin lands, when you notice which outcome you were secretly hoping for.

IntuitFlip does not tell you what to do. It helps you notice what you already feel.

The Problem: decision fatigue

Modern decision fatigue rarely comes from missing information. It comes from overthinking: comparing endlessly, simulating outcomes, researching more, and delaying action.

The coin flip works as a psychological probe. By temporarily removing control, it creates a brief moment where clarity leaks out.

The Mechanism

  1. Enter two options.
  2. Flip the coin.
  3. Notice your first emotion.
  4. Select: relieved or disappointed.
  5. Receive a short reflective insight.

The coin is random. Your reaction is not.

Psychology behind IntuitFlip

  • Anticipatory Emotion: We feel outcomes before rationalizing them.
  • Cognitive Dissonance: Discomfort appears when outcome and desire conflict.
  • Somatic Signals: The body reacts faster than analysis.

Philosophy

  • Stoic acceptance of uncertainty.
  • Existential responsibility: you choose, not fate.
  • Randomness reveals preference; it does not dictate action.

Design Approach

The interface is intentionally restrained: dark minimal visuals, subtle glassmorphism, tactile coin animation, and no clutter.

No gamification, no notifications, no growth hacks. The experience is meant to feel more like a desk object than a startup dashboard.

Future Direction

  • Expand the ritual coin collection.
  • Introduce limited-edition digital and physical coins.
  • Offer subtle pattern analysis without surveillance.
  • Maintain minimalism as a core constraint.

Growth matters less than integrity.

Closing

Sometimes clarity is not a matter of logic. It is a matter of noticing.

IntuitFlip simply gives that moment room to appear.
