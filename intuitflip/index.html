Project

🪙 IntuitFlip

A subconscious decision-making ritual.

Live React Tailwind Framer Motion PWA Local-first
Open IntuitFlip ↗ ← Back to portfolio

Overview

IntuitFlip is a minimalist decision ritual disguised as a coin flip. At first glance, it looks simple: you enter two options, flip a coin, and receive a result.

But the result is not the point.

The real signal happens in the split second before the coin lands — when you realize which outcome you’re secretly hoping for. IntuitFlip captures that moment. It doesn’t tell you what to do. It reveals what you already wanted.

At a glance

  • Type: Progressive Web App (PWA)
  • Live at: intuitflip.com
  • Stack: React · Tailwind · Framer Motion
  • Data: Local-first, no accounts
  • Status: Live, evolving

The problem

Modern decision fatigue doesn’t come from lack of information. It comes from overthinking.

We compare endlessly. We simulate outcomes. We research. We delay. But often, emotionally, we already know. The coin flip is a psychological probe — it temporarily removes control, and that’s when clarity leaks out.

If the coin lands on “Pizza” and you feel disappointed, the answer was never Pizza.

The mechanism

The flow is intentionally ritualistic.

  1. Enter two options. Name the decision without overthinking the labels.
  2. Flip the coin. A tactile animation with physical weight and presence.
  3. Notice your first emotion. Before logic kicks in, observe the gut reaction.
  4. Select: relieved or disappointed. A single honest signal.
  5. Receive a short, reflective insight. Generated locally — to translate emotion into language, not to advise.

The coin is random. Your reaction is not.

Psychology behind it

IntuitFlip is built around three simple ideas.

⚡ Anticipatory emotion

We feel outcomes before we rationalize them. The body responds to imagined futures in real time.

🔀 Cognitive dissonance

When the result conflicts with our desire, discomfort appears instantly — before any deliberate thought.

🫀 Somatic signals

The body often reacts faster than logic. Instead of suppressing that reaction, IntuitFlip isolates it.

It creates a pause long enough to observe the signal that was always there.

Philosophy behind it

IntuitFlip borrows quietly from ideas that predate the digital age:

  • Stoic acceptance of uncertainty
  • Existential responsibility — you choose, not fate
  • The idea that randomness reveals preference, it doesn’t dictate action

The core truth

The coin does not decide.

You do. The coin only removes the illusion of neutrality — the belief that more analysis will eventually produce the right answer.

Design approach

The goal was to design something that feels closer to a desk object than a startup tool.

🌑 Dark, minimal UI

Every element earns its place. Nothing decorative unless it adds meaning.

🪟 Subtle glassmorphism

Depth without noise — translucent surfaces that recede rather than compete.

🪙 Tactile coin animation

Framer Motion physics give the coin physical weight. The flip feels earned.

🚫 No clutter

No gamification. No notifications. No streaks. No social loops. Just the ritual.

Why I built it

I’ve built many tools that optimize productivity, automate tasks, or gamify systems. This one does the opposite.

It slows you down.

I wanted to explore whether technology could create clarity instead of more noise. IntuitFlip is small on purpose — it doesn’t try to be a life system. It creates a moment.

Technical stack

  • Frontend: React, Tailwind CSS
  • Animation: Framer Motion
  • Data: Local-first, no backend
  • Delivery: PWA — installable, offline-capable

Future direction

Growth matters less than integrity here.

  • Expand the ritual coin collection
  • Introduce limited-edition digital and physical coins
  • Subtle pattern analysis — without surveillance
  • Maintain minimalism as a hard constraint, not a guideline

IntuitFlip simply gives that moment room to appear.
